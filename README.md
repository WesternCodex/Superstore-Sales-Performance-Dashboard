# Retail-Sales-Performance-Dashboard


## Overview

This project is an interactive **Power BI dashboard** built to analyze retail sales performance and uncover meaningful business insights. It explores sales, customers, products, and shipment performance to help identify trends, measure business growth, and support better decision-making.

The dashboard was designed to answer key business questions such as:

- How profitable is the business?
- Which products generate the most revenue?
- Are customers returning to make repeat purchases?
- Which shipping methods do customers prefer?
- What opportunities exist to improve performance?

---

## Project Objectives

The goal of this project is to:

- Analyze overall sales and profit performance.
- Understand customer purchasing behavior.
- Identify top-performing and low-performing products.
- Evaluate order and shipment trends.
- Provide data-driven recommendations for business improvement.

## Key Insights

### Sales Performance

The business generated **₹2.29M** in total revenue and **₹284.46K** in total profit, resulting in a **12.44% profit margin**. This is well above the typical retail profit margin, showing that the business is performing strongly.

Sales and profit increased significantly during the **October to December** festive season, showing that demand is highly seasonal.

The **Technology** category performed the best, contributing:

- **36.53%** of total revenue
- **51.23%** of total profit

This makes Technology the most valuable product category for the business.

---

### Customer Insights

Although Technology had the fewest customers, it generated the highest revenue. This shows that customer spending is more important than customer volume.

Customer retention is a major challenge.

Out of **4,865 customers**, only **94** made another purchase, giving a **Repeat Customer Rate of just 1.93%**.

Customer breakdown:

- 98.07% purchased only once.
- 92 customers purchased twice.
- Only 2 customers purchased three times.

The business currently depends more on attracting new customers than keeping existing ones.

---

### Product Insights

Sales are concentrated among a small number of products.

While many products perform well, **92 out of 1,850 products** were purchased only once during the entire period.

This suggests that some products may not be adding much value and could be reviewed for better inventory management.

---

### Orders & Shipment Insights

Orders and shipments followed similar trends throughout the year, showing that operations are keeping up with customer demand.

Most customers preferred **Standard Class Shipping**, suggesting they value lower delivery costs over faster shipping options.

---

## Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the business should consider:

- Investing more in Technology products since they generate the highest profit.
- Introducing customer loyalty programs to increase repeat purchases.
- Reviewing low-performing products and focusing inventory on high-demand items.
- Preparing inventory and logistics ahead of peak festive seasons.
- Expanding successful sales strategies into other regions.
- Continuing to improve Standard Class shipping to maintain customer satisfaction while controlling costs.
